Background

Ascendas SCM signed a 12-month integrated contract (NexSEO $1,800/mo + PPC $2,500/mo + Reddit $1,000/mo = $5,300/mo + GST). The client ran for approximately 1.5 months before going MIA around 13 Nov 2025 — no termination notice was provided during any exit window. The cancellation review revealed three process breaks within FPD's own operations, complicating full fee enforcement.

Where the process broke
Break 01
PPC card activated before kick-off
Performance MediaSalesNo policy
Sales pushed Performance Media to activate at SA signing. AM Carmen had not yet been assigned and had zero visibility. No written policy defined when activation was permitted. Finance discovered this during the cancellation review — months after the fact. Nobody owned the step because no policy made anyone own it.
Break 02
Stop work date incorrectly recorded
AM
AM Tansy logged 13 Nov as the stop work date — the date the last outbound task was sent. Tech continued active work with no stop instruction. CRM wasn't updated until December after contact attempts failed. This discrepancy complicated the termination fee calculation and required reconciliation with the tech team.
Break 03
Reddit delivery not tracked or escalated
AMFinance
Client paid ~3 months ($3,000). Only 5 of 30 contracted posts were delivered. No delivery shortfall was flagged during the campaign. Partial delivery on Reddit weakened FPD's position — you cannot pursue complete fee enforcement while having materially underdelivered.
Learnings by department
Account Management
L1
You own the campaign record — not just client comms
Stop work dates and campaign status in the CRM must reflect actual operational reality. If tech is still working, the campaign is still running. Update the CRM when delivery has actually ceased — not when client contact stopped.
L2
Credit card and vendor activation is your authorisation to give — in writing
No card or vendor cost should be activated before you, the AM, send written confirmation that the campaign is live. Sales does not own this step. Performance Media cannot act without your instruction. This protects both the client relationship and FPD's recoverable position if a cancellation occurs.
L3
Delivery tracking is your defence in a dispute
Subcontractor outputs must be tracked and confirmed against contracted scope monthly — not reconstructed during a cancellation review. Delivery shortfalls must be escalated before they become part of a termination dispute. Partial delivery weakens every claim FPD makes.
L4
Handovers must include full account state — assume nothing was communicated
Every Sales-to-AM handover must include all vendor costs already activated, invoices raised, and commitments made. AM Carmen had no visibility over the credit card activation because it happened before she was assigned. If it is not documented in the handover, treat it as unknown.
Sales
L1
Closing the deal does not authorise vendor or card activation
SA signing is not a green light for any operational cost to be incurred. Sales does not have the authority to instruct Performance Media to activate credit cards or vendor accounts. That instruction belongs to the AM, after campaign go-live is confirmed. Pushing teams to act early creates unrecoverable exposure if the campaign is cancelled shortly after.
L2
The handover to AM is a structured brief, not a verbal mention
When a contract is signed, Sales must formally brief the assigned AM on everything committed during the sales process — timelines, platforms, any pre-activation that occurred, and client expectations set. An incomplete handover creates accountability gaps that only surface during escalations.
Performance Media
L1
No written instruction from AM = no activation
Performance Media must not activate credit cards, vendor accounts, or any cost-incurring resource based on verbal direction from Sales or assumption that approval exists. The rule is simple: written instruction from the assigned AM, on or after confirmed campaign go-live date, is the only valid trigger. If that instruction hasn't arrived, hold and escalate.
L2
Stop work instructions must come in writing — and be actioned immediately
When a client goes MIA and the AM signals concern, Performance Media needs a formal written stop instruction before halting delivery. Without it, work continues and costs accumulate. Flag to the AM if no stop instruction has been received within a reasonable period of the client going quiet — don't wait to be told.
Tech / Delivery
L1
Active work without a live campaign creates unrecoverable cost
The tech team continued working on NexSEO tasks (keyword research, prompt research, tracking setup) after the client went MIA, with no stop instruction received. Work done during an unconfirmed pause period cannot be billed and becomes an internal write-off. If a stop instruction hasn't been received and the client has gone silent, flag it — don't continue.
L2
Task completion in Sentr is the source of truth for stop work dates
The discrepancy between the AM-logged stop work date (13 Nov) and actual tech activity in Sentr (ongoing after that date) required reconciliation during the cancellation review. Tech activity logs are a record — they will be checked in disputes. Work cessation in Sentr must align with the official stop instruction, and any divergence should be flagged immediately.
Finance
L1
Credit card activation dates should be reconciled against campaign go-live at billing
The pre-kickoff card activation (23 Sep vs. 10 Oct go-live) was only discovered during the cancellation review. A routine check — confirming that card activation date aligns with the campaign go-live date on file — at first invoice would have caught this immediately and allowed for early correction or write-off before it became a legal complication.
L2
Subcontractor AP costs must be matched to contracted delivery before payment
The $250 Ruglas AP payment for Reddit was processed without confirmation of how many posts had been delivered against the 30 contracted. AP payments to vendors should be matched to a confirmed delivery count from the AM before release — especially for scope-based deliverables where partial completion is possible.
